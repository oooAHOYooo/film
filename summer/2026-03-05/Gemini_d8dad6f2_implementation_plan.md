# Implementation Plan: Option 3 & Expanding the Runtime

You liked the idea of staging the final battle out in the hostile environment of the tall grass rather than the house. This makes the finale much more rugged, claustrophobic, and cinematic.

However, we need to address the runtime. **The current script compilation runs about 58 pages (roughly 58 minutes).** To hit your goal of a 90-minute movie, we need to add about 30 pages of material. The climax is the perfect place to build out that length by dramatically expanding the tension, the journey, and the obstacles in the marsh.

Here is a beat-by-beat breakdown of how we can expand Scenes 22 through 25 into a massive, 30-minute Third Act.

## Phase 1: The Tactical Load-Out & The Breach (Expands Scene 22)
*Instead of just running into the grass, this becomes a major sequence.*
1. **The Breach:** The Red-Eyes don't just wait at the edge of the property; they actively attack the house. The crew is trapped inside while the power goes out. The hum rattles the foundation.
2. **The Realization:** Makayla realizes firing the phase-canceler inside will kill them. The only way to survive is to push into the marsh. 
3. **The Load-Out:** They have to physically dismantle the trap they just built on the porch. Dallas and Dominic strap car batteries to their backs. Makayla creates a portable harness for the oscillator. It’s a tense, heavy preparation sequence.

## Phase 2: The Trench Run (Expands Scene 23)
*Walking through the grass isn't easy; it takes time, physical effort, and stealth.*
1. **The Sensory Deprivation:** Once they enter the tall grass, it's pitch black and the howling wind is deafening. They have to move slowly, almost blind, relying entirely on Makayla's blinking green LED.
2. **The Cat and Mouse:** They have to avoid patrolling Red-Eye shadows. They use the portable speaker to create small, temporary "bubbles" of safety, but the battery starts draining fast.
3. **The Mud and Breakdown:** Dominic gets stuck in the marsh mud. The heavy speakers are sinking. They have a moment of complete despair before Asher steps up, using his connection to the creature to guide them forward.

## Phase 3: The Elders & The Ambush (Expands Scene 24)
*The discovery of the Elders becomes a major emotional set-piece.*
1. **The Sanctuary:** They find the "geometric ring" of flattened grass that the Golden Elders made. It’s quiet here. The storm doesn't reach them. We take time to see the Elders interacting with Asher and the injured creature.
2. **The Swarm:** The Red-Eyes find the ring. It’s not a quick attack; it’s a sustained siege. The Elders use their golden mist to fight back, and we see an actual battle of frequencies (light vs shadow). 
3. **The Sacrifice:** The Elders are overwhelmed. They physically shield the crew and the injured creature, giving them time to finish wiring the heavy speakers.

## Phase 4: Firing the Phase Canceler (Expands Scene 25)
*The final blast is earned through physical struggle.*
1. **The Malfunction:** In the mud and rain, the copper wire snaps. Makayla has to rewire the crystal bare-handed while a massive shadow bears down on her.
2. **Dominic's Stand:** The Red-Eyes charge the speakers. Dominic has to use his own body weight to hold the heavy wooden frames upright in the mud, sinking deeper with every impact.
3. **The Final Note:** Dallas slams the dial. The concussive blast of 115.3 MHz detonates inside the claustrophobic walls of the grass, vaporizing the shadows in a massive shockwave of golden light.

## The Path Forward
If we expand these four phases with detailed action, tense dialogue, and physical struggle, we can easily add 20-30 pages to the third act, pushing the script toward that 90-minute runtime while making the ending significantly more epic.
