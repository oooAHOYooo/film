# Scene 27B: The Acoustic Kill Zone
<!-- summary: The climax. The storm hits, the power goes out, and the shadow creatures arrive. The phase-cancellation trap is tested. Sparks of golden static as the acoustic wall holds them back. -->

EXT. DALLAS'S BACKYARD - NIGHT

The storm is at its peak. Siding rattles. The neighborhood is pitch black, stripped of power. 

Dallas, Makayla, Asher, and Dominic stand on the porch, staring out at the yard. The only light comes from the small, blinking green LED on Makayla’s sensor, wired between the two massive speakers facing the marsh.

DOMINIC
(shouting over the wind)
I can't see anything! We should go to the basement!

MAKAYLA
If we go down there, we trap ourselves! It has to hold here!

Suddenly, the wind drops. The rain is still falling, but the howling stops. 

Then it starts. **THE HUM.**

It rises from the marsh, a dense, low-grade vibration that rattles Dallas’s teeth. Slowly, at the edge of the tall grass, the darkness seems to thicken. 

ASHER
(pointing into the void)
They're coming.

Pairs of red eyes blink into existence. Two. Then four. Then a dozen. They aren't walking—they are drifting, heavy and crushing, erasing the sound of the rain as they move closer to the yard. 

DOMINIC
(panicking)
What is that?! What IS THAT?!

Dallas doesn't answer. He grips the railing. He watches his military receiver. The waveform on the screen is a jagged, violent mess. 

DALLAS
(to Makayla)
The frequency is spiking. Wait for the corridor. 

The red eyes glide past the edge of the property line. The veins of burn marks on the grass begin to hiss, turning pitch black. The shadows form into horrific, massive silhouettes, reaching out toward the house. 

MAKAYLA
Now! Dallas, hit it!

Dallas slams the master switch on the oscillator. 

A high-pitched, harmonic tone—**115.3 MHz**—blasts through the speakers. It isn’t just loud; it's physical. 

The leading shadow creature stretches a massive, formless tentacle toward the yard. 

It hits the space between the speakers. 

**CRACK!**

A brilliant flash of golden static erupts in mid-air. The shadow recoils, letting out a horrific gnashing sound. It didn't hit a wall—it hit the acoustic kill zone. The opposing frequencies collide, creating a dome of sheer sonic force.

DOMINIC
(covering his ears)
It's an invisible wall! 

More shadows rush the perimeter. Snap! Crack! Flashes of golden electricity light up the backyard like a strobe as each shadow hits the phase-cancellation trap and is violently repelled. The air smells of ozone and burnt ozone.

Asher watches, mesmerized. He taps his leg twice. It’s working.

But the receiver in Dallas’s hand starts to smoke. The needle is buried in the red. 

DALLAS
The crystal! It’s drawing too much current!

MAKAYLA
Hold the line! If we drop the frequency, they'll erase us!

The largest of the shadows rears up, its red eyes burning bright. It throws its entire weight against the center of the acoustic corridor. The speakers groan. The golden static flares so bright it blinds them. 

The oscillator whines—a desperate, failing sound. 

DALLAS
(screaming)
Hold on!

Dallas grabs the dial and forces it perfectly onto **115.3**, reinforcing the signal with everything the battery has left. 

A massive, concussive **BOOM** of golden light shatters outward. The shockwave knocks Dallas and Dominic backward onto the porch. 

Silence. 

Total silence. 

Dallas slowly gets up. His ears are ringing. He looks out at the yard. 

The red eyes are gone. The shadows are gone. 

Only the gentle sway of the tall grass remains, illuminated by flashes of natural lightning. Between the two smoking speakers, the blackened burn marks on the grass are slowly fading back to green. 

Makayla lets out a breath she’s been holding for what feels like hours. Asher smiles. 

DOMINIC
(stunned, out of breath)
What... what did you just do?

DALLAS
(looking at the smoking receiver)
We tuned them out.
