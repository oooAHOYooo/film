# Script Review: Creatures in the Tall Grass

Overall, you are doing **fantastic**. The story has a wonderful *Stranger Things* meets *A Quiet Place* vibe, centered completely around a unique mechanic—bio-acoustics. The way you’ve woven Dallas's grief over his late wife Sierra with the literal frequencies of the Golden Elders is a beautiful emotional anchor. The climax in the marsh with the heavy 80lb speakers works incredibly well visually. 

Here are some things that are working well, and a few areas for improvement (things that bumped me as a reader).

## What’s Working Expertly
- **The Core Mechanic:** Using waveforms, frequencies, and oscillators to repel monsters and heal the earth is highly original and very cinematic. 
- **The Physicality of the Climax:** The decision to haul massive literal eighty-pound wooden speakers into the mud added so much physical weight and tension. Dominic physically anchoring the tipping speaker as the rot infects him is a great, heroic moment.
- **The Sound Design on the Page:** You do a great job describing the hum, the silence, and the concussive "BOOM" of the golden light. The auditory experience is very clear in the script.
- **Emotional Payoff:** The ending where Dallas realizes the creature's frequency matches Sierra's breathing cadence brings everything full circle beautifully.

## Areas for Improvement & "Bumps"

### 1. The LifeGroup Tension Deflation (Scene 18-19)
You build up an incredible amount of tension with Dominic hosting the LifeGroup upstairs and Dallas hiding an injured (and loud) alien creature right downstairs. The storm is brewing, windows are rattling, and people are getting suspicious. 

**The Bump:** Right at the peak of this tension, the LifeGroup just... leaves peacefully. ("In time, people leave. Hugs. Goodbyes.") 
**Advice:** Have the climax interrupt the end of the LifeGroup! Let the Red-Eyes attack the house while the guests are still there, forcing Dallas and the kids to expose their secret to save everyone—or have the storm get so violently terrifying that the guests flee in a panic. Don't let the tension fizzle out before the marsh battle.

### 2. Makayla's Age & Dialogue
**The Bump:** In Scene 1, Makayla is established as 22 and Asher as 14. However, Makayla often speaks and acts like someone much younger (saying things like "Hey mister", "Byeeee", and having a homemade "hideout" in the woods). If she's 22, the way she commands the tech makes complete sense, but her attitude reads more like a 15-16 year old.

**Advice:** If you want her to be a 22-year-old, maybe age up her dialogue a bit to make her sound more like a tech-savvy adult taking care of her little brother. If you want her to be younger, maybe drop her age to 17 or 18 (so she can still build the tech but fit the "teens exploring" trope better).

### 3. Lore Drops (Mr. Mike & Javarice)
**The Bump:** In Scene 7 and Scene 27, Mr. Mike drops mentions of "Javarice" (or Javerias?). This comes across as a very sudden deep-lore drop that isn't really explained anywhere else in the script.

**Advice:** If Javarice is important to the history of the Golden Elders or the town, maybe have Dallas ask who that is, or have Dominic explain a bit of Mr. Mike's backstory natively. If it isn't crucial, consider simplifying it so the audience isn't trying to solve a puzzle that doesn't have an answer in this movie.

### 4. Minor Continuity and Typos
- **Scene 3:** The bird app is called Merlin, but Dominic calls them "marlins". Similarly, "offspree" should be "osprey". If this is intentional character voice, make it explicitly clear he's getting the words wrong!
- **Scene 7:** The location header says `INT. JACE'S BASEMENT` instead of `DOMINIC'S BASEMENT`.
- **Character Relations:** Clarify Janice's exact relation. Is she Dominic's wife or sister? The kids call him "Uncle" and her "Aunt Janice", but in Scene 14 Makayla says "Our mom died too... Janice was her sister, Dominic is her younger brother." But earlier (Scene 7) Dominic talks about his late wife. Just make sure the family tree is locked down completely.
