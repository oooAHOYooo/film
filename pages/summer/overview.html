The Story

A bioacoustics researcher moves to a coastal Connecticut town for a fresh start, only to pick up a signal from the marshes that shouldn’t exist. When he follows the sound into the reeds, he discovers something injured—and something else that is hunting it. A grounded sci-fi thriller about sound, solitude, and what we find when we finally stop to listen.

Story Breakdown

A brief treatment and detailed act structure.

Creatures in the Tall Grass is a slow-burn sci-fi thriller that grounds extraterrestrial horror in the quiet melancholy of a grieving family. Act I establishes the mystery as bioacoustics researcher Dallas picks up anomalous frequencies from the local marsh. In Act II, the mystery deepens when Dallas and his niece discover a frail, glowing alien mimic using sound as camouflage. Act III unleashes the tension when apex predators—massive shadows known as "Red-Eyes"—surround the property to claim the creature, forcing the family to mount a desperate defense. Finally, Act IV delivers an explosive acoustic showdown where the family weaponizes military-grade oscillators to create a localized sonic collapse, obliterating the threat and finally allowing themselves a chance to heal.

Act I: The Frequencies

Dallas Demartini, mourning the loss of his wife Sierra, moves into his brother Dominic's home in coastal Connecticut. While setting up his vintage audio equipment to continue Sierra's bioacoustics research, Dallas intercepts erratic, unidentifiable frequencies from the nearby tall grass. The ambient tension rises as strange weather phenomena hit Branford, and the local wildlife falls silent. The act culminates when Dallas, aided by his sharp-witted niece Makayla, tracks the signal to a geometric clearing in the marsh.

Act II: The Mimic

Dallas recovers a frail, glowing creature and smuggles it into the basement. The narrative pivots from mystery to tense incubation as they discover the creature is a "mimic," learning to replicate specific radio frequencies (115.3 MHz) as a defense mechanism against a looming threat. As Dallas and Makayla try to nurse it, the creature's singing inadvertently acts as a beacon. Subdued paranoia mounts during a crowded living-room LifeGroup meeting upstairs, where the floorboards literally vibrate with the creature's growing power.

Act III: The Trap

The secret is out. Dominic discovers the truth just as the apex predators—towering, shadow-like entities known as the "Red-Eyes"—breach the property line, leaving corrupt, creeping burn marks in their wake. With the house surrounded and the storm raging, the family realizes they cannot run. Using Sierra’s old research notes, Makayla reverse-engineers a localized phase-canceling acoustic beam that temporarily repels the creatures. The family frantically works through the rain to drag heavy, military-grade speakers into the yard to build a defensive acoustic perimeter.

Act IV: Point Source Interference

The climax is an intense auditory war. The Red-Eyes swarm the acoustic corridor, violently tearing through the protective soundscape. In a daring move, Dallas dives into the reeds to save the creature from the onslaught. Using raw copper wire and volatile frequencies, they trigger a massive, invisible acoustic collision that weaponizes sound itself, vaporizing the attacking shadows. In the haunting, quiet aftermath, the creature reunites with its elders in the marsh, leaving the broken family fundamentally changed—and finally ready to look forward.
