# 3D Shot Generator Guide: The Connection (S14)

This guide focuses on setting up the two primary environments for Scene 14: Dallas's backyard picnic area and his kitchen triage lab. There's a lot of emotional and technical acting here.

## 1. Build the 3D Environment (The Set)
*You'll need an indoor/outdoor setup. Toon Boom will handle the details, so keep meshes simple.*
- **The Backyard**: Spawn a flat grass plane. Place a long rectangular box scaled to represent the old wooden picnic table.
- **The House Exterior/Interior Wall**: Spawn a wall separating the yard from the kitchen. Punch out a large hole for the kitchen window/glass door so the characters inside can look out into the tall grass later.
- **The Kitchen**: On the indoor side, spawn a large box to serve as the Kitchen Island, and maybe a smaller counter box against the wall for Asher's makeshift computer setup.
- **Lighting**: Start with a flat, slightly overcast "daytime" ambient light. As the scene progresses and the storm/wind picks up, you can drop the ambient light and use a directional light pointing through the kitchen window to cast dramatic shadows.

## 2. Cast & Props
- **The Cast**: Spawn one female model (Makayla), one adult male model (Dallas), and an adolescent/younger male model (Asher).
- **Howie (The Dog)**: If you have a dog/quadruped model, spawn him placing him near Dallas's feet at the picnic table. Otherwise, use a small box to mark his space.
- **The Creature**: Place a small prop (a tiny box or organic shape) on the Kitchen Island. Spawn a *warm point light* hovering just over it to represent the heat lamp.
- **Scientific Gear**: Use small boxes or tablets on both the counter for Asher and the island for the oscilloscope/receivers.
- **The Crystal Prop**: Attach a small, bright blue/white point light near Makayla's hand when she plugs the crystal into the amplifier.

## 3. Posing the Action (Shot by Shot)

### Shot 1: The Backyard Conversation (Wide)
- **Camera**: Set camera to `24mm` lens. Place it in the yard looking at the picnic table.
- **Pose Makayla & Dallas**: Sit them on top of or next to the picnic table. Pose Dallas looking slightly down or off into the distance. 
- **Pose Asher**: Place Asher a few feet away, seated on the ground (use a "Sit" or "Cross-legged" pose). Place a notebook prop in his hands.

### Shot 2 & 3: Asher's Overload (Medium to Shaky Close-up)
- **Camera**: Move inside the kitchen. `35mm` lens, pointing at the wall counter.
- **Pose Asher**: Use a "Standing/Tinkering" pose with his hands near the cables. For Shot 3, switch to a "Fetal" or "Collapsed on Floor" pose.
- **Lighting Tip**: Angle the main directional light through the window so it hits Asher hard, mimicking the sudden change in the atmosphere.

### Shot 4: Makayla's Comfort (Wide Low Angle)
- **Camera**: Drop the camera straight down to the kitchen floor looking across the tiles.
- **Action Posing**: Pose Makayla kneeling, draped over Asher. Tilt her head down toward his chest.

### Shot 6 & 7: The Kitchen Lab (Medium to Close)
- **Camera**: Raise the camera back up, aiming at the Kitchen Island. `50mm` lens for a tighter, more intimate feel.
- **Pose the Trio**: Stand Makayla, Dallas, and Asher grouped closely around the island. Use "Investigating" or "Leaning Forward" poses in the Shot Generator so they all look intently down at the island center.
- **Dallas's Action**: Position Dallas's hand hovering over the receiver prop to adjust the dial.

### Shot 8: The Crystal Amplifier (Insert)
- **Camera**: `85mm` macro on the island.
- **Action Posing**: Just Makayla's hands reaching into the frame, inserting the bright glowing point light (the crystal) into the box. 

### Shot 10: The Realization (POV Wide)
- **Camera**: `24mm` or `35mm`. Position the camera behind the backs of the characters or right from the island looking *out* the big kitchen window.
- **Action Posing**: Turn all three characters' heads uniformly away from the island to look out the glass towards the tall grass. 

## 💡 Pro-Tips for the 3D Generator
1. **The Oscilloscope Screen**: Don't try to model the jagged sine waves in 3D. Just use a flat, dimly glowing green plane as a placeholder. You'll draw the colliding sine waves directly over it in Toon Boom or Storyboarder’s 2D layer.
2. **Glass & Glare**: Since they are looking from the dark inside to the bright outside through a window, frame the characters in partial silhouette in Shot 10 to emphasize whatever is happening outside in the grass.
