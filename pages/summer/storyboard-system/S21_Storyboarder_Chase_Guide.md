# 3D Shot Generator Guide: Makayla Runs into the Backyard (S21)

This guide focuses purely on the chaotic sequence where Makayla sprints out the door into the storm, followed by the boys, and the frantic backyard action.

## 1. Build the 3D Environment (The Set)
*Keep it simple so you can pose fast. You will draw over this in Toon Boom.*
- **The House Exterior**: Spawn a basic wall to represent the back of Dallas’s house. 
- **The Porch & Door**: Spawn a raised platform (a scaled box) and a doorway opening. You don't need a physical door unless you want to pose them pushing through it. 
- **The Yard/Marsh**: Leave a large flat plane for the grass. 
- **Lighting**: Drop the ambient world light down very low (it's night/storming). Add a strong, chaotic "moonlight" or "storm light" (directional or spotlight) casting long, harsh shadows across the yard.

## 2. Cast & Props
- **Makayla**: Spawn your female character model. 
- **The Boys (Dominic, Dallas, Asher)**: Spawn three male models. 
- **Flashlight Prop (CRITICAL)**: Attach a flashlight prop to Makayla’s hand. In the Shot Generator, **attach a spotlight to the flashlight prop**. This is what will drive the dramatic lighting of her running.
- **The Swarm/Red Eyes**: Place small red point lights clustering at the far edge of your yard plane. You don't even need character models for them yet—just the glowing red lights to establish the eyeline.

## 3. Posing the Action (Shot by Shot)

### Shot 1: The Sudden Exit (Handheld Tracking)
- **Camera**: Set camera to `35mm` lens. Place it just inside or right on the threshold of the door, looking out.
- **Pose Makayla**: Select Makayla, use the built-in "Run" or "Sprint" preset pose. Angle her lunging aggressively off the porch into the darkness. 
- **Lighting Tip**: Aim her flashlight directly forward so we see the beam cutting through the dark yard.

### Shot 2: Bursting Into the Storm (Low Angle Tracking)
- **Camera**: Drop the camera height down to waist/knee level on the porch, pointing out toward the yard.
- **Pose the Boys**: Place Dominic, Dallas, and Asher clustered tightly in the doorway. Use "Run" or "Lunge" poses for them, bursting out *after* Makayla. 
- **Depth**: Put Makayla further into the background (Z-depth) so she feels distant and moving fast.

### Shot 3: Swarm Approaches (Medium Push-in)
- **Camera**: Move the camera out into the yard, pointing *back* at the porch (or profile on the porch stairs). 
- **Pose Dallas & Asher**: Stand them on the edge of the porch, squinting into the dark. Use a "Defensive" or "Bracing" pose against the wind.
- **The Threat**: Move your cluster of red point lights noticeably closer to the porch to show the swarm closing the distance.

### Shot 4: The Push Back (The Speaker / Crystal)
- **Camera**: Wide shot showing the whole porch.
- **Action Posing**: Drop Makayla to her knees (use a "Kneel" preset). Spawn a box prop on the railing to act as the speaker. Pose Dallas ripping wires (head near hands) and Asher with hands overlapping the equipment.

## 💡 Pro-Tips for the 3D Generator
1. **Duplicate Boards**: Once you set up the porch, the characters, and the lighting—**don't build it again.** Just hit ` + ` to duplicate the storyboard panel. Your 3D set and characters carry over, then you just move the camera and adjust the poses.
2. **Rain Effects**: Don't try to build rain in the 3D generator. Add it as a quick 2D sketch layer right over the 3D models using Storyboarder’s pencil tool to give the boards that chaotic storm energy. 
3. **Field of View (FOV)**: For the aggressive running shots, lower your camera's focal length (e.g., `24mm` instead of `50mm`). It warps the perspective slightly and makes the characters look like they are running *faster* and the space feel *larger*.
