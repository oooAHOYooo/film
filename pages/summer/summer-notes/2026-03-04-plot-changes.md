# Script Timeline & Plot Revisions

Here is a full log of all the structural and dialogue changes made to smooth out the timeline. You can reference this when you go back through to humanize the voices and add your own specific flavor to the dialogue:

### 1. Merging Scene 17 & 18 (The Basement Discovery)
* **What changed:** `s17.md` was completely deleted and its contents were folded directly into the beginning of `s18.md` (and `s17` was removed from the `manifest.json`).
* **Why:** Having two separate scenes in the exact same location breaking up the discovery felt disjointed.
* **What you can humanize:** In `s18`, the scene now starts with Makayla checking her viewfinder (tracking the Red-Eyes swarming Creek Bend), noting that the creature's erratic breathing matches the jagged lines on the screen, before Asher locks in the `115.3` frequency so the creature can mimic it. You can adjust the flow of Makayla and Dallas's whisper-shouting here to sound more natural.

### 2. Scene 15 (Makayla's Lair / Battery Issue)
* **What changed:** At the very end of `s15`, right after Asher wakes up from his trance, an action beat was added where the oscilloscope battery suddenly dies. Dallas points out they need a dedicated power source (like a wall plug) to run the phase-canceler. Simultaneously, Makayla gets an angry text from Aunt Janice telling them to get inside before the storm hits.
* **Why:** This provides the critical missing link for why they take the military rig and the injured creature from the Lair (where they built it) straight into a crowded LifeGroup setting. They were trying to stop at Dallas's house for power out of necessity.
* **What you can humanize:** You can tweak Makayla and Dallas's frantic back-and-forth about the dead battery and Aunt Janice's text message to fit their voices better.

### 3. Scene 16 (The Trap Is Set)
* **What changed:** The ending portion of `s16` was rewritten so the crew explicitly walks to Dallas's house. Aunt Janice's dialogue was changed from saying "we're doing life group at my house, stay down there" to "my house is completely off-limits!" On top of this, Dominic hops out of his minivan with trays of food, revealing to a horrified Dallas that *Dallas* had previously offered to host LifeGroup, which Dallas had completely forgotten. 
* **Why:** This creates the "lock-in" trap. It organically justifies why they have to hide the creature in the basement through scenes 18 and 19 while a house full of people sing upstairs. 
* **What you can humanize:** You can play up Dominic's cheerful, oblivious energy as he unloads the food, contrasting with Dallas's absolute horror that ten families are about to walk into his "makeshift hospital." 

### 4. Scene 29 (The Lair's Return at the End)
* **What changed:** The short snippet in `s29` about Makayla and the kids finding a caterpillar near the church was deleted. Instead, a brand new ending scene for her was written set back at the Lair. It shows Asher stringing up massive new trail antennas, and Makayla testing the crystal on a new device. She gets a clean sine wave, puts on headphones, and says, *"Okay. Let's hear what you have to say."*
* **Why:** This brings Makayla's arc full circle, satisfying the desire to return to the Lair later in the plot. It turns the Lair from a one-off set piece into her official "research base" for communicating with the creatures moving forward. 
* **What you can humanize:** You can expand on the new tech they are building, or tweak Makayla's final line to be whatever profound thought you want her to go out on!
