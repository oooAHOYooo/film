INT. DALLAS'S LIVING ROOM - NIGHT

The house is packed. Families sit on folding chairs, eating Dominic’s Brussels sprouts. TYLER, a young man with a guitar, hands out music sheets.

TYLER

Let’s start with something to settle the nerves. The storm’s close.

They begin to sing a hymn. As the twenty voices rise in harmony, the hum from the basement begins to harmonize with them. It’s subtle at first, then undeniable.

The floorboards begin to vibrate. The guests look around, confused.

PAT CLENDENEN

(the weatherman)

Must be the wind hitting the eaves. Pressure's dropping fast.

But Dallas is staring at the basement door. The sound isn't the wind. It’s an answer.
