Creatures in the Tall Grass

Music

Original songs in film order — from Dallas's first day in Branford through the acoustic collapse and what's left after.

The Guiding Principle

Sound is the film's central argument. The music should feel like it was picked up by Dallas's equipment rather than dropped in from outside — sparse, low-frequency, built from resonance rather than melody. The score lives in the body before it reaches the ear. Songs earn their place by belonging to the same acoustic world as the creature's hum.

# Song Scene Mood & Direction
Act I — The Frequencies
01
"Arrival"
 Dallas unloads boxes, looks at the tall grass for the first time Arrival without excitement. Dallas is here because he had nowhere else to go. The grass is vast and still and he is small in front of it. No percussion — just something that breathes.
02
"The Hum"
 Dallas unpacks Sierra's bioacoustics equipment in the spare room Every piece of equipment is a relic. He handles it accordingly. Grief that has gone quiet — not raw, just present. Slow and patient, with a tone that lingers a beat longer than expected.
03
"The Coordinates"
 Dallas picks up the anomalous frequency alone at the equipment, late at night The signal isn't frightening yet — just wrong in a way he can't explain. Puzzled, pulled forward, a little electric. A pattern that almost resolves but doesn't.
04
"Marsh Walk"
 Makayla leads Dallas toward the marsh at dusk — the first walk in Makayla moves through the grass like she knows it. The feeling of being led somewhere by someone younger and more certain than you — wonder and mild unease together. The song moves. Not urgent, but forward.
Act II — The Mimic
05
"The Hollow"
 The geometric clearing — Dallas steps into the ring of flattened grass and sees the creature Not fear — the suspension just before fear, the moment before your body decides what it is. The air pressure changed. Something resonant and low-frequency, as if the ground itself is the instrument.
06
"The First Hum"
 The creature in the basement — its first sustained, unbroken hum fills the room The creature sings to survive, not to communicate. But it is also genuinely beautiful, and that's the tension. Wonder with an undercurrent of dread. A melody learned from a signal, not from music.
07
"The Hymn"
 LifeGroup meeting upstairs — neighbors singing together while the creature pulses through the floor below Domestic horror — the ordinary made thin. A hymn that's come slightly unmoored, something communal and warm that the circumstances have made uncanny. It sits under the scene, not over it.
Act III — The Trap
08
"The Burn Mark"
 The Red-Eyes breach the property line — first burn marks at the fence in the rain They don't announce themselves. The burn marks are the announcement. Bass that arrives low and stays low — not a swell, not a sting. The threat is patient. The music should be too.
09
"Shadows in Wind"
 The family drags military-grade speakers through the yard in the storm Physical labor under terror. Moving enormous equipment through mud and rain while shadows pace the property line. Desperate momentum — focused urgency when the only option is work. The song has mass. It feels like something being dragged.
Act IV — Point Source Interference
10
"Final Echoes"
 The acoustic perimeter fires — the sonic collision begins and the Red-Eyes are destroyed Sound as weapon. Dallas fires a machine built from his grief — Sierra's research, her frequencies. Release after extreme tension, but not triumphant. More like a dam breaking. Frequency against frequency until one gives.
11
"Little Aftermath"
 The creature glows at the edge of the reeds and returns to the marsh The yard is quiet. The creature holds at the fence line, then disappears. Nobody speaks. The specific stillness after something enormous has passed through — not relief exactly, more like the world resettling.
12
"Makayla's New Lair"
 End credits — Makayla sets up in the woods; Dallas finds Sierra's hum in the marsh Not resolution so much as permission — to keep going, to look ahead. The oscillators are still in the spare room and he will use them again. Quiet but not defeated. Something that opens rather than closes.
