Creatures in the tall grass

Table Read

February 22, 2026

"Something's in the marsh. A hum. Then something more."

Dallas moves to Branford with a box he won't open—Sierra's field notes, her sketches, the receiver they used together. Next door, his neighbor Dominic's niece and nephew want his oscillator; something's moving under the reeds, and the power grid's acting up. When Dallas finds an injured creature in the tall grass, Makayla finds out. So does the thing that's chasing it. A small-town fellowship, a life group in the living room, and a return to the hollow.

Character Resonance

Dallas

Before: Records sound to escape the quiet of grief. Silence reminds him of death.

After: Gains a "new sense." His auditory cortex restructures. He can distinguish the individual layers of the world's orchestra.

Makayla

Before: Fearless and open. No baggage. A clean signal.

After: The frequency enters her bones. She moves from curiosity to fierce protection. She leads the chord.

Dominic

Before: Fills silence with activity—cooking, talking, organizing. Stillness is where his loss lives.

After: Learns that silence isn't absence; it's full. He finds the ability to sit in a space that isn't empty anymore.

The Laws of the Marsh

The Chord Protects

A Singer alone is vulnerable. Singers together are safe. Connection protects.

Silence is a Weapon

The Eaters don't roar; they consume vibration. When they approach, the world goes mute.

The Grass is the Body

The marsh doesn't just house them; it's a symbiotic waveguide. The grass sings back.

Story Overview

Act I — Arrival & Discovery (Scenes 1–11): New house, tall grass, the hum. Dallas meets Makayla and Asher; fellowship, marsh walk, news vans. The coordinates, the hollow, the creature in the kitchen.

Act II — The Creature (Scenes 12–23): First night with the creature, Makayla confronts Dallas, bond and lair. Creature in the basement; life group arrives, Mr. Mike freaks. Red eyes, the creature gone.

Act III — The Return (Scenes 24–27): Marsh plan, predator attack, the perimeter. Creature dies in the marsh; they run. The plan, the trap, the storm.

Act IV — Aftermath (Scenes 28–29): She goes in. Phase cancellation; they're free. Branford breathes again; the folder's with the kids.
